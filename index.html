Sim2Real Project

This project focused on sim-to-real transfer for robotic control using NVIDIA Isaac Sim and Isaac Lab. I developed a digital twin of the UR3 robotic arm and the Robotiq 2F-85mm gripper in Isaac Sim, integrated with Isaac Lab for reinforcement learning (RL). Through this setup, I gained hands-on experience in training an RL policy using a Markov Decision Process (MDP) in Python to perform a pick-and-place task.

The trained reward-based policy successfully demonstrated the target behavior in simulation. While partially successful in real-world deployment, I identified opportunities for improvement through advanced techniques such as dual-policy architectures or imitation learning to enhance policy performance and generalization.

For deployment, the trained policy would be transferred from simulation to the physical robot using the joint observations as well as ROS to interface to the UR3. Due to location between the robot and simulation I could not achieve a direct interface from simulation to the robot. Instead I opted to develop TCP socket communication method to communicate between the simulation pc (client) and to the a device (server) to connect to the robot itself remotely using TCP sockets over a VPN. This led to successful control over the physical robot itself using my trained policy. A video example can be found in the repository.

Key Skills: Python, Reinforcement Learning, TCP Sockects, ROS, Sim2Real
